# Hello, I'm Su Min (Michelle)!

* 🎓 I'm pursuing my **Master's in Computational Social Science** at the **University of Chicago**, and completed my B.A. in **Data Science & Sociology** at UC Berkeley — a combination that pretty much sums up how I think about everything.
* 🔍 My work sits at the intersection of **AI ethics, computing education, and computational social science**. Drawing on Bourdieu, Gramsci, and Kimberlé Crenshaw, I study how AI ethics discourse functions as a hegemonic knowledge project, how capital structures what counts as legitimate ethics, how academia reproduces industry framing through consent, and how even critical interventions can reinscribe the hierarchies they aim to dismantle. I'm currently a Graduate Research Assistant at the **Knowledge Lab** (PI: James Evans), and previously co-authored four posters at SIGCSE TS 2025 on student engagement in computing ethics education.
